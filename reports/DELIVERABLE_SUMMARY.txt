✨ PROJECT SUMMARY

This comprehensive analysis of Sino-Kazakh bilateral trade (2015-2024)
documents a fundamental structural shift occurring in 2023. After eight
consecutive years of healthy trade surpluses averaging $5.1 billion
annually, the relationship reversed with deficits of $4.0 billion (2023)
and $3.8 billion (2024).

The shift was driven by asymmetric growth: Kazakhstan's imports from China
surged 263% while exports only grew 120%. Three sectors (machinery,
vehicles, textiles) experienced dramatic import increases (424-932%), while
supply chains concentrated heavily on Chinese sources (77-90% penetration).

The document presents this structural shift through:
  • 13 professional annotated visualizations
  • 4 comprehensive statistical tables
  • 5 detailed key findings
  • Clear Kazakhstan-centric interpretation
  • Policy implications and conclusions
